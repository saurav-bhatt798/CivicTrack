How CivicTrack Works

Simple steps from reporting to resolution

1

Report Issue

Citizen takes photo of issue, adds description, and pins location on map. Submission takes under 2 minutes.

2

System Verification

AI checks for duplicates, categorizes issue, and assigns priority level. Complaint appears on public map.

3

Authority Assignment

Municipal admin reviews and assigns to relevant department. Status changes to "In Progress".

4

Resolution & Feedback

Department resolves issue, uploads completion photo. Citizen gets notification and can rate the service.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about using CivicTrack

How do I report an issue?

Click "Report Issue" button, select category (road, street light, garbage, etc.), upload photo, add description, pin location on map, and submit.

Do I need to create an account?

Yes, you need to sign up to report issues and track your complaints. This helps us prevent spam and allows us to notify you about status updates.

How long does it take to resolve issues?

Average resolution time is 18 hours. Emergency issues (like broken water mains) are prioritized and typically resolved within 6 hours.

Can I report anonymously?

For tracking and follow-up, we require registration. However, your personal details are not displayed publicly on the complaint map.
